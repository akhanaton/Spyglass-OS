# Tutor Outreach Template

A partnership-oriented message to an independent maths tutor. Lead with value for them, not for us. Low-commitment first ask.

## Structure

**Subject line (email only)**
Short, specific, no marketing fluff.
- "Quick question about your [Cambridge 9709 / Edexcel IAL] students"
- "A tool for your [exam board] students (not a tutor replacement)"

**Opening (1-2 sentences)**
Show you know who they are. Reference their platform, the exam board they teach, something specific. Never generic.

**Problem (1-2 sentences)**
Surface the pain they deal with: tracking student progress between sessions, students not doing enough practice, not knowing where each student's weak spots are.

**What ExamPilot does for their students (2-3 sentences)**
Outcome-focused. Not features.
- "Your students get personalised gap analysis between sessions"
- "It maps their weak topics across [paper code] and adapts the revision plan as they improve"
- "You'd get a dashboard showing exactly where each student needs help"

**What's in it for them (1-2 sentences)**
- Their students perform better (reflects on them)
- They save time tracking progress
- They can recommend something credible

**The ask (1 sentence)**
Low-commitment. Not "sign up" or "buy". Examples:
- "Would you be open to having 2-3 of your students try it for free? I'd love your feedback."
- "I'd value 15 minutes of your time to show you how it works. No pitch, just a demo."
- "If you're interested, I can set up free accounts for a few of your students this week."

**Sign-off**
Name, role at ExamPilot. Keep it human.

## Rules

- Never position as a tutor replacement. Tutors reject anything that threatens their livelihood.
- Lead with THEIR value, not ours
- WhatsApp: only with warm intro or prior engagement elsewhere
- Maximum 1 follow-up if no response
- No more than 1 message per tutor per month
- No bulk sending. Each message is personalized.
- Voice matches references/voice-enitan.md for personal outreach
- [VERIFY] any claims about dashboard or tutor-facing features (they may not be built yet)

## Referral Angle (for follow-up conversations)

Once a tutor is engaged:
- They refer students, students perform better, tutor looks good
- Offer affiliate/referral arrangement if appropriate (log decision in decisions/log.md)
- Track referred students in pipeline so we can report back to the tutor
