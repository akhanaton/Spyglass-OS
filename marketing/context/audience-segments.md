# Audience Segments

Messaging angles per segment, ordered by acquisition priority. For WHO these segments are and validation methodology, see wiki `customer-validation.md`. This file covers HOW to talk to each.

**Segment priority order:** Cambridge 9709 Student (primary user) > Parent (economic buyer) > Tutor (individual referral) > Teacher/School (institutional referral) > Edexcel IAL Student > Resit Student.

## Cambridge 9709 Student

**Exam board:** Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics (9709)
**Paper codes to reference:** 9709/12, 9709/13 (Pure 1), 9709/32, 9709/33 (Pure 3)
**Geography:** International students. EU, UAE, Pakistan, Malaysia, Singapore, Nigeria.

**Pain points to surface:**
- "I don't know what I don't know" — can't identify weak topics until the exam
- Past paper grinding with no feedback on WHY answers are wrong
- Static PDF resources (PapaCambridge, SaveMyExams) that don't adapt
- No way to track progress across topics over time
- Private tutoring is expensive or unavailable in their region

**Language register:** Casual, peer-level. Not teacher-to-student. These are 16-18 year olds.

**Messaging angle:** "Know exactly where you stand on 9709 Pure 1. ExamPilot maps your weak topics and builds a revision plan that adapts as you improve."

**What NOT to say:** "AI-powered", "revolutionary", "game-changing". Students see through marketing speak instantly.

## Edexcel IAL Student

**Exam board:** Edexcel International Advanced Level Pure Mathematics
**Paper codes:** WMA11 (Pure 1), WMA12 (Pure 2)
**Geography:** Similar to Cambridge but with stronger presence in South Asia and Middle East.

**Key difference from Cambridge:** Different syllabus structure, different paper format. Content must be specific to Edexcel, not generic "A-Level Maths".

**Messaging angle:** "Built for WMA11 and WMA12. Not generic maths. Every question mapped to Edexcel's actual specification."

## Tutor

**Who:** Independent maths tutors teaching Cambridge 9709 or Edexcel IAL. Found via LinkedIn, tutor directories, WhatsApp groups.

**What they care about:** Tools that make their students perform better (reflects on them). Time savings in tracking student progress. Something they can recommend that's credible.

**Messaging angle (partnership):**
- Lead with value for THEM: "Your students get personalised gap analysis between sessions. You get a dashboard showing exactly where each student needs help."
- Low-commitment first ask: "Would you be open to having 2-3 of your students try it for free? I'd love your feedback."
- Referral angle: they refer students, students perform better, tutor looks good.

**What NOT to say:** Don't position as a tutor replacement. Tutors will reject anything that threatens their livelihood.

## Parent (Economic Buyer)

**Who:** Parents of Cambridge/Edexcel students. The economic buyer in most target markets (Pakistan, UAE, Nigeria, Malaysia, Singapore). Skews older (40-55). Making or approving the purchase decision.

**What they care about:** Is my child making progress? Is this safe/trustworthy? Is it worth the money vs. a private tutor (EUR40-80/hour)? Will it help with university admission?

**Pain points to surface:**
- Can't see what their child is actually doing in "revision time"
- Private tutoring is expensive (USD 25-80/hour) and hard to assess
- No way to track progress between parent-teacher meetings
- Concerned about online safety and screen time quality
- School fees already high (EUR2,100-37,000/year) -- need to justify additional spend

**Messaging angle:**
- Trust and safety first: "Built for under-18s. GDPR compliant. No social features. Pure study tool."
- Value framing: "From EUR12/month with an annual plan. That's one hour of private tutoring for a year of daily practice."
- Progress visibility: "See exactly where your child stands and what they need to work on."

**Language register:** Professional-parental. Not student casual, not corporate. See `references/voice-parent.md`.

**Channel (primary acquisition path):**
- Facebook Groups: COALI (100K+ members, Pakistan-dominant), Cambridge International Education page (969K+ likes), Dubai Expat Community (110K+), Abu Dhabi Q&A (93K+), school-specific parent groups
- WhatsApp parent groups in Tier 1 markets (school-level groups, exam prep groups)
- "For Parents" landing page on exampilot.io (trust-first design, value framing, progress visibility)
- Parent email sequence via Brevo (4 emails: trust, product, social proof, trial CTA)
- Indirect via student ("Mum, I need this for revision")

**What NOT to say:** Guilt framing ("Don't let your child fall behind"). AI-forward language (parents are more skeptical of AI in education). Pressure tactics. Comparison with other parents.

## Teacher / School (Referral Partner)

**Who:** Maths teachers and heads of department at CIE/Edexcel international and private schools. Found via LinkedIn, teacher WhatsApp groups, education conferences, warm intros from existing tutor contacts.

**Relationship:** B2C2B referral partner, not customer. ExamPilot is always consumer-purchased. Teachers recommend. Students/parents buy individually. No school licensing, no institution pricing.

**What they care about:** Tools that make their students perform better (reflects on them). Time savings in tracking student progress. Something credible they can recommend without risking their professional reputation.

**Pain points to surface:**
- Hard to track individual student progress across 20+ topics between lessons
- Limited visibility into what students actually do outside class
- Want to identify class-wide weak topics to prioritise in teaching
- Time pressure -- need tools that work without admin overhead

**Messaging angle (B2C2B partnership):**
- Lead with value for THEM: "Your students get personalised gap analysis between sessions. You get a dashboard showing exactly where each student needs help."
- Zero friction: "Share one link. Students sign up individually. No admin on your end."
- Low-commitment first ask: "Would you be open to having 2-3 of your students try it for free? I'd love your feedback."
- Referral mechanics: 20+ students via teacher's link = extended free access + recognition

**Language register:** Professional, collegial. See `references/voice-teacher.md`.

**Channel:**
- LinkedIn (Teresa's personal account — content warm-up required before any DM outreach; see `build-in-public/references/linkedin-strategy.md`)
- Teacher WhatsApp groups (professional groups, not personal)
- Education conferences and school events (Tier 2+)
- Warm intros from existing tutor contacts
- Pipeline tracked in Attio CRM

**What NOT to say:** B2B pricing/licensing language ("school plan", "per-seat"). Don't position as tutor/teacher replacement. Don't imply their current teaching is insufficient. Don't cold spam.

## Resit Student (Post-August)

**Who:** Students who received results in August 2026 and are resitting in October/November 2026. First-mover opportunity after Results Day.

**Emotional state:** Disappointed, stressed, under time pressure. They know what they got wrong. Very different from first-time students.

**Messaging angle:**
- Urgency: "Oct/Nov is 10 weeks away. ExamPilot identifies your exact weak spots and builds a targeted revision plan."
- Specificity: "You know your grade. We know your gaps. Let's close them."
- No shame: Never reference failure or poor results. Frame as "improvement" and "next step".

**Channel:** Reddit (r/alevel results day threads), Discord, targeted email to existing users who didn't convert.

**Timing:** Results Day August 2026 is the trigger. Content and outreach templates should be ready before that date.
