# AI Citation Targets

Last updated: 2026-05-16

## Why this matters

When students and parents ask AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude) questions about A-Level Maths revision, those tools cite sources. ExamPilot needs to be one of those cited sources. This is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — the discipline of making content that AI systems trust, extract, and recommend.

The goal is not to game AI systems. It is to produce content that is so useful, specific, and well-structured that AI systems have no better source to cite for exam-prep questions about Cambridge 9709 and Edexcel IAL.

**Target: 10 AI citations tracked and verified by end of Month 3 (Foundation phase end).**

## GEO Optimization Requirements

For content to earn AI citations, every targeted article must meet these requirements. These come from `marketing/references/geo-platform-guide.md` — this is the short-form checklist.

- **Answer-first:** The main query must be answered in the first 1-2 sentences of the page or section. Not a teaser — the actual answer.
- **Key Takeaways block:** Present immediately after introduction. 3-4 standalone claims with specifics.
- **FAQPage schema:** Minimum 4 Q&A pairs. Answers under 200 characters for AI Overview extraction. Natural language questions (not keyword-stuffed).
- **Full entity names:** "Cambridge International AS & A Level Mathematics 9709" on first mention (not "Cambridge A-Level" or "CIE"). "Edexcel International Advanced Level Pure Mathematics WMA11" on first mention.
- **Primary source citations:** Link to Cambridge Assessment specification URL and/or Pearson specification URL. These signals tell AI systems the content is grounded in official sources.
- **Original data or insight:** Something no other page has — a specific comparison, a worked example, data from ExamPilot's own usage patterns (when available). Unique content earns citations.
- **Structured content:** Tables, numbered lists, definition blocks, step-by-step blocks. AI systems extract structured content more reliably than flowing prose.
