## Direct Competitors (same solution, same audience)

Competitors offering exam prep, revision, or practice tools for A-Level Maths students taking Cambridge or Edexcel qualifications.

---

### 1. SaveMyExams (savemyexams.com)

**What they are:** The dominant revision resource for UK and international A-Level students. Covers all major exam boards with topic summaries, past paper questions, and mark scheme explanations. Membership model with free tier.

**Strengths:**
- Very high brand recognition in the UK A-Level market
- Wide topic coverage across all subjects, not just maths
- Well-structured, clean content that students trust
- SEO dominance — ranking for most A-Level Maths terms
- Free tier exists (lower activation barrier)

**Weaknesses:**
- Static content — the same notes and questions for every student
- Passive consumption — students read and watch, but the content doesn't adapt to what they don't know
- No feedback on why an answer is wrong (just the mark scheme)
- Generic across exam boards — not Cambridge-specific in depth
- No spaced repetition or knowledge mapping

**SEO profile:**
- Domain authority: Very High [VERIFY exact DA]
- Top content types: Topic summaries, past paper questions, worked solutions, revision checklists
- Keyword clusters owned: "A level maths revision", "cambridge past papers", "[topic] exam questions", "GCSE maths revision" (broader market)
- Estimated organic traffic: Very high [VERIFY]

**ExamPilot's angle:**
"SaveMyExams gives you everything. ExamPilot shows you what you don't know yet. One is a reference library; the other is a practice engine that adapts to your gaps."

**Where they appear in comparison searches:**
- "SaveMyExams vs [alternative]" — they are the benchmark. Students use them as the default and look for reasons to switch.
- Opportunity: Students who use SaveMyExams and still feel underprepared are our most qualified audience.

---

### 2. PapaCambridge (papacambridge.com)

**What they are:** A massive archive of Cambridge past papers, mark schemes, and examiner reports. Free. No login required. The go-to for raw past paper access.

**Strengths:**
- Enormous content library — past papers going back decades
- Free, no registration
- Cambridge-specific depth (unlike SaveMyExams, which covers many boards)
- High trust among Cambridge students internationally
- Massive indexed content = high SEO authority for paper code + year searches

**Weaknesses:**
- No explanations — just the paper and mark scheme
- No active learning — pure passive resource
- Poor UX — functional but not designed for modern study habits
- No progress tracking, no personalisation
- No search or topic-based navigation (you navigate by year/session)

**SEO profile:**
- Keyword clusters owned: "cambridge 9709 past papers", "CIE mark scheme [year]", "[paper code] specimen paper"
- ExamPilot does NOT compete for these keywords directly — past paper repositories are not our content type

**ExamPilot's angle:**
"PapaCambridge gives you the papers. ExamPilot turns them into active practice. We explain why each answer earns its mark, not just what the mark scheme says."

---

### 3. Physics & Maths Tutor (physicsandmathstutor.com)

**What they are:** A well-established UK revision site covering A-Level Maths and Physics with revision notes, worked solutions, and video walkthroughs. UK-focused (primarily OCR, Edexcel UK, AQA) but covers some Cambridge content.

**Strengths:**
- High-quality worked solutions
- Video content alongside text (serves visual learners)
- Well-organised by topic and exam board
- Strong domain authority
- Trusted by UK sixth-form teachers and tutors

**Weaknesses:**
- Less depth on Cambridge 9709 compared to UK boards — this is their weak point for ExamPilot's audience
- Not adaptive — one set of solutions for all students
- No personalisation or knowledge mapping
- Videos can feel long relative to the specific insight students need

**SEO profile:**
- Keyword clusters owned: "a level maths worked solutions", "maths revision notes", "[topic] worked examples"
- Strong on generic A-Level Maths terms but weaker on Cambridge paper-code-specific terms

**ExamPilot's angle:**
"Physics & Maths Tutor shows you how to solve problems that have already been solved. ExamPilot trains you to recognise patterns and solve unseen questions — what the examiner actually tests."

---

### 4. Revision Village (revisionvillage.com)

**What they are:** IB (International Baccalaureate) maths revision platform. Similar product concept to ExamPilot — topic-specific questions, worked solutions, videos.

**Why listed:** Not a direct competitor (different qualification), but a reference product that shows what a well-executed exam prep tool looks like for international students. Students who use Revision Village for IB Maths SL/HL are exactly the type who would use ExamPilot for 9709.

**Lessons from their model:**
- Paper-specific content (HL Paper 1, SL Paper 2) is highly valued by students — confirms our approach
- Video solutions are a major draw — worth considering for Phase 2 content
- Community and social features build retention — monitor as Phase 2+ feature

## Content Competitors (different solution, overlapping keyword space)

These do not offer a competing product, but they rank for keywords ExamPilot wants to own. They shape what students see in SERP before finding ExamPilot.

---

### 5. Reddit (r/alevel, r/6thForm, r/CambridgeInternational, r/Edexcel)

**Why relevant:** Students ask exam questions here and get peer answers. High community trust. Reddit threads rank for long-tail conversational queries — exactly the queries ExamPilot wants to be in.

**ExamPilot opportunity:**
- Answer questions in these subs genuinely (using `/write-reddit`)
- Build credibility over time — when ExamPilot has a reputation in the community, organic mentions follow
- Monitor for high-volume questions that signal content gaps

**SEO note:** Reddit results appearing for our target keywords are not content we can beat with a standard article — they appear because of domain authority. The play is to be the cited expert in those Reddit threads, not to outrank them.

---

### 6. The Student Room (thestudentroom.co.uk)

**Why relevant:** UK-focused student forum. Ranks for many "help with [topic]" queries. Students turn to it when stuck. Less international than Reddit.

**ExamPilot opportunity:** Low — primarily UK domestic focus, heavy A-Level (UK syllabus) not Cambridge International. Monitor but do not prioritise for outreach.

---

### 7. YouTube (tutoring and revision channels)

**Why relevant:** Some target keywords trigger video carousels in SERP. Channels like "Cambridge A Level Maths Revision" and individual tutors have growing audiences.

**ExamPilot opportunity:** Not in current channel mix (TikTok planned for Phase 2, not YouTube). Flag for Phase 3 strategy review. Awareness only: students who watch YouTube revision content are in our target audience.

## ExamPilot's Defensible Position

**We are NOT:**
- A repository of past papers (that's PapaCambridge — they have a decade head start and it's free)
- A set of static notes (that's SaveMyExams — they have massive content library and SEO authority)
- A video tutorial channel (that's Physics & Maths Tutor — video production is a different capability)

**We ARE:**
- The active practice layer that makes all of the above stick
- The only tool that knows what a specific student doesn't know yet
- Built specifically for 9709 and Edexcel IAL — not adapted from a generic maths tool
- Feedback-rich: we explain why, not just what the answer is

**The product gap we fill:** Students use SaveMyExams and PapaCambridge and still fail. They know the theory but can't apply it under exam conditions. ExamPilot is where knowing becomes doing.
