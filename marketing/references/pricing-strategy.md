# Pricing Strategy

Pricing frameworks and research methods for ExamPilot. Current pricing is set (EUR29/mo, EUR69/3mo, EUR96/6mo, EUR144/yr) but this reference supports future pricing decisions, tier changes, and pricing page optimization. Adapted from marketingskills pricing skill.

## Current ExamPilot Pricing

| Plan | Price | Per month | Savings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly | EUR29/mo | EUR29 | — |
| Quarterly | EUR69/3mo | EUR23 | 21% |
| Semi-annual | EUR96/6mo | EUR16 | 45% |
| Annual | EUR144/yr | EUR12 | 59% |

**Model:** 14-day trial (no CC required) then subscription. No free tier. EUR only. B2C consumer.

**Value metric:** Flat fee (not per-seat, not usage-based). Appropriate for individual student use.

## The Three Pricing Axes

### 1. Packaging (what's included at each tier)
ExamPilot currently has one tier (all features). Consider tiering if:
- Different student segments need different feature sets
- Tutors need a dashboard (different value prop = different tier)
- Usage-based limits make sense (e.g., daily practice sessions)

### 2. Pricing Metric (what you charge for)
Current: flat monthly fee. Alternatives to evaluate:
- Per exam board (pay for Cambridge 9709, add Edexcel IAL separately)
- Per subject (when expanding beyond maths)
- Usage-based (number of SRS sessions per day)

### 3. Price Point (how much)
Current pricing was set based on competitive positioning vs. private tutoring (EUR40-80/hour). Validate with research methods below.

## Value-Based Pricing

Price between the floor (next best alternative) and ceiling (perceived value):

- **Perceived value ceiling:** Private tutoring at EUR40-80/hour. ExamPilot delivers daily practice for EUR12-29/month.
- **Next best alternative floor:** Free resources (PapaCambridge, SaveMyExams PDFs) at EUR0. ExamPilot's adaptive features justify the gap.
- **Current positioning:** "One hour of tutoring buys a year of daily practice" (EUR144/yr annual plan).

## When to Change Pricing

### Signs it's time to raise

- Prospects don't flinch at price
- Conversion rate is very high (>40% trial-to-paid)
- Monthly churn is very low (<3%)
- Significant value added since launch (new exam boards, new features)
- Competitors have raised prices

### Signs it's time to restructure

- Different segments have wildly different willingness to pay
- Students want Cambridge 9709 only but are paying for Edexcel too (or vice versa)
- Tutor dashboard is ready (different value prop, different pricing)
- Expanding beyond A-Level maths to other subjects

### Price increase strategies

1. **Grandfather existing:** New price for new customers only
2. **Delayed increase:** Announce 3-6 months out, give time to lock in current rate
3. **Tied to value:** Raise price but add features/exam boards
4. **Plan restructure:** Change plans entirely (e.g., add tiers)

## Pricing Research Methods

### Van Westendorp (Price Sensitivity Meter)

Four questions to identify acceptable price range:
1. "At what price would ExamPilot be too expensive to consider?"
2. "At what price would ExamPilot be so cheap you'd question its quality?"
3. "At what price is it starting to get expensive but you'd still consider it?"
4. "At what price would it be a bargain?"

**How to run:** Survey 100-300 students/parents. Segment by exam board and geography. Plot cumulative distributions. Key intersections reveal optimal price point and acceptable range.

**When to run:** After 100+ paying users. Before any price change.

### Gabor-Granger (Willingness to Pay)

"Would you pay EUR[X]/month for ExamPilot?" (Yes/No)

Vary price across respondents to build a demand curve. Simpler than Van Westendorp, works well for validating a specific price point.

### MaxDiff (Feature Valuation)

Show sets of 4-5 features. Ask: "Most important? Least important?" Repeat across sets. Results rank features by perceived value, informing tier packaging.

**Use for:** Deciding which features go in which tier if we add tiers.

## Pricing Page Best Practices

- Recommended tier highlighted (visual emphasis)
- Monthly/annual toggle with annual savings called out
- Show EUR savings, not just percentages
- FAQ section addressing "is it worth it vs. free resources?"
- Social proof: student results, testimonials (with consent, parental consent for under-18s)
- Money-back guarantee reduces risk perception
- Comparison to tutoring cost (EUR40-80/hour) for parent audience

### Pricing Psychology

- **Anchoring:** Show annual plan first (lowest per-month cost)
- **Decoy effect:** Quarterly plan makes semi-annual look like better value
- **Loss aversion:** "Save EUR204/year with annual plan" (show what they'd lose on monthly)
