# Content Repurposing Playbook

How to turn one piece of ExamPilot content into multiple formats across channels. Plus short-form video strategy for TikTok (P2). Adapted from marketingskills social skill + short-form-video + post-templates references.

**When to use this:** When repurposing existing content across channels. Future `/repurpose` command (P3) will reference this guide. Also useful for `/write-reddit` when adapting blog content for Reddit.

## The Repurposing Principle

One blog article should produce 5-10 derivative pieces across channels. This is not copy-paste. Each format requires genuine adaptation to the channel's native style.

## Blog Article → Everything Else

Starting from a single SEO blog article (written via `/write-article`):

| Derivative | Channel | Adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| Value post | Reddit | Extract one insight. Rewrite in peer voice. No CTA unless asked. |
| Comment replies | Reddit | Use article knowledge to answer questions in target subs |
| Email tip | Email list | One key insight + link to full article |
| TikTok script | TikTok | One surprising fact or tip. 30-60 seconds. Visual. |
| Carousel | Instagram (future) | 8-10 slides, one point per slide, bold text |
| Thread | Twitter/X (future) | 5-7 tweets, one point per tweet, hook in tweet 1 |
| Student DM resource | WhatsApp/Discord | "Hey, wrote up something on [topic] that might help: [link]" |

### Adaptation Rules

- Reddit: rewrite completely in student voice. Remove all marketing language. The value must stand alone without ExamPilot.
- Email: lead with the insight, not the article. Link is secondary.
- TikTok: one hook, one idea, one CTA. 30-60 seconds max. See video section below.
- Never cross-post the same text to multiple platforms.

## Short-Form Video Strategy (TikTok — P2)

### ExamPilot Video Hooks

**Curiosity hooks (best for engagement):**
- "The topic that trips up 70% of Cambridge 9709 students"
- "Nobody talks about this Paper 3 mechanics trick"
- "I can't believe this actually works for integration..."

**Value hooks (best for saves):**
- "How to revise [topic] in 10 minutes"
- "3 mistakes that cost you marks on [paper]"
- "If you're struggling with [topic], watch this"

**Story hooks (best for watch time):**
- "I went from a D to an A in 3 months. Here's what changed."
- "My tutor told me something about [topic] that changed everything"

### ExamPilot Video Script Template

```markdown
## Video: [Working Title]

**Platform:** TikTok
**Length:** 30-60 seconds
**Format:** [Talking head / Screen recording / Whiteboard]

### Hook (0-3 sec)
- Visual: [What viewer sees]
- Audio: [What they hear]
- Text overlay: [On-screen text]

### Body (3-50 sec)
- [Timestamp] - [What happens / what you say]
- [Continue...]

### CTA (final 3-5 sec)
- Verbal: [What you say]
- Text: [On-screen text]
- Action: [Follow, comment, link in bio]
```

### Video Structures

**The Quick Tip (15-30 sec):**
```
[0-3s]  Hook: Bold claim or question
[3-20s] Tip: The actual advice with visual
[20-30s] CTA: "Follow for more A-Level tips"
```

**The Story Arc (45-60 sec):**
```
[0-3s]  Hook: Tease the outcome
[3-15s] Setup: Context and stakes
[15-45s] Journey: What happened / the method
[45-55s] Resolution: The result
[55-60s] CTA
```

**The Myth-Bust (30-45 sec):**
```
[0-3s]  Hook: "Everyone says [common belief]"
[3-10s] Setup: Why it seems true
[10-35s] Reality: What actually works and why
[35-45s] CTA
```

### Posting Strategy

| Metric | Growing phase | Maintaining |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 1/day | 3-5/week |
| Best times (UK) | 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-11 PM | Test and adjust |

**Batch creation workflow:**
1. Ideate (30 min): 10 concepts from recent blog articles and Reddit questions
2. Script (1 hour): Write 5-10 scripts
3. Film (2 hours): Batch all talking head content
4. Edit (2-3 hours): Add captions and overlays
5. Schedule (30 min): Queue for optimal times

### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Watch time % | Is content engaging throughout? |
| Completion rate | Did hook + content deliver? |
| Saves | Is content valuable enough to revisit? |
| Shares | Is content worth spreading? |
| Profile visits → follows | Did viewer want more? |
| Link clicks (bio) | Conversion to site |

## Social Post Templates

### Reddit Value Post (expanded from template)

```
[Hook: Relatable student pain or question]

[Body: Genuinely helpful content. Share what you know.
Structure as numbered tips or clear explanation.
Write as a peer, not a brand.]

[Optional soft mention: Only if ExamPilot is directly relevant
to what you're explaining. Never in the first post.]
```

### Email Newsletter Tip

```
Subject: [2-4 words, lowercase, topic-focused]

[One insight from a recent article, in 2-3 sentences]

[Why this matters for their revision]

[One specific thing they can do today]

[Optional: "Full article here: [link]"]
```

### Student DM (resource share)

```
Hey [name], saw your question about [topic].

[One helpful insight or tip — under 40 words]

[If relevant: "I put together a guide on this: [link]"]

Good luck with the revision!
```

## Hook Formulas (Cross-Platform)

### Curiosity
- "The real reason [outcome] happens isn't what you think."
- "Nobody talks about [insider knowledge]."
- "[Result] and it only took [surprisingly short time]."

### Value
- "How to [outcome] without [common pain]:"
- "[Number] mistakes that are costing you marks on [paper]:"
- "The simplest way to [outcome]:"

### Contrarian
- "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]"
- "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why:"
- "I stopped [common practice] and [positive result]."

### Story
- "3 months ago, I [bad state]. Today, I [good state]."
- "I made a huge mistake with [topic]."
- "My teacher told me something I'll never forget."

## Content Calendar Integration

Repurposing should be systematic, not ad hoc:

| Day | Primary content | Derivatives |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Blog article published | — |
| Tuesday | — | Reddit value post adapted from article |
| Wednesday | — | Email tip extracted from article |
| Thursday | — | TikTok script from article's best insight |
| Friday | `/weekly-pulse` | Review what performed, plan next week |

This cadence means 1 blog article/week produces 3-4 channel-specific pieces.
