# Outreach Benchmarks & Sequences

Performance benchmarks, personalization systems, follow-up sequences, and subject line data for ExamPilot outreach. Adapted from marketingskills cold-email skill, reframed for B2C student/tutor outreach (DMs, email, WhatsApp).

**When to use this:** When drafting outreach via `/outreach-draft` or evaluating outreach performance. Commands load this on demand.

## Performance Benchmarks

### Industry Context

ExamPilot outreach is B2C (students, parents, tutors), not cold B2B sales. These benchmarks come from B2B cold email but set a useful floor. B2C outreach to warm/semi-warm contacts (Reddit engaged users, tutor referrals) should outperform these.

| Metric | B2B Average | B2B Good | ExamPilot target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 27.7% | 40-45% | 50%+ (warm contacts) |
| Reply rate | 4-5.8% | 5-10% | 10-15% (warm) |
| Positive reply % | ~48% | 55-60% | 60%+ |
| Bounce rate | 7.5% | <4% | <2% |

### EdTech Specifically

EdTech reply rates average 7.8% (above SaaS at 3.5%). ExamPilot's niche targeting (specific exam boards, specific pain) should push higher.

### Declining Effectiveness Trend

Reply rates dropped from 7-8% (2020-2022) to 4-5.8% (2024-2025). Drivers: inbox saturation, AI email flood, stricter anti-spam. Writing craft matters more, not less. The gap between average and excellent is widening.

## Personalization System

### The Rule

If your personalization has nothing to do with the problem you solve, it's just an attention hack. The personalization must logically connect to the revision/exam problem.

### Four Levels

| Level | What it is | ExamPilot example | Lift |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Basic | First name, school | "Hi {{name}}" | ~5% (table stakes) |
| 2. Segment | Exam board, year group | "Most Cambridge 9709 students struggle with..." | Moderate |
| 3. Role | Student vs tutor vs parent | Different pain points, different register | Good |
| 4. Individual | Specific observation tied to their problem | "Saw your post about Paper 3 mechanics. That topic trips up most students because..." | 50-250% lift |

### Research Signal Stack for ExamPilot

| Signal | Where to find it | How to use it |
|---|---|---|
| Reddit post/comment | r/alevel, r/6thForm | "Saw your question about [topic]. Here's what most students miss..." |
| Exam board | Post content, flair | Tailor to Cambridge 9709 or Edexcel IAL specifically |
| Exam timing | Calendar, post urgency | "With May/Jun exams X weeks away..." |
| Specific topic struggle | Their question content | "Integration by parts trips up most students because..." |
| Results day context | August posts | "Resits are stressful. Here's what actually helps..." |

### The "So What?" Test

After writing any opener, read from the student's perspective: "So what? Why would I care?" If the answer is nothing, rewrite.

### What Feels Fake (avoid)

- Generic: "I saw your Reddit post and wanted to reach out"
- Disconnected flattery: "Cool profile!" then pitch
- Over-personalizing to creepiness
- AI-generated phrasing ("I hope this message finds you well")

## Follow-Up Sequences

### The Data

- 55% of replies come from follow-ups, not the initial message
- First follow-up adds 49% more replies
- By 4th follow-up, response rates drop 55% and spam complaints triple
- 48% of outreach never gets a single follow-up

### ExamPilot Outreach Cadence

Cap at 2 follow-ups for student DMs (3 total). Tutors can get 3 follow-ups (4 total). Increase the gap between touches.

**Student DM sequence:**

| Touch | Day | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Initial DM | 0 | Personalized value tied to their question/need |
| Follow-up 1 | 3-4 | Different angle: share a resource, tip, or insight |
| Follow-up 2 | 7-10 | Soft close: "No worries if not for you. Good luck with [exam]." |

**Tutor outreach sequence:**

| Touch | Day | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Initial email | 0 | Value for their students + low-commitment ask |
| Follow-up 1 | 4-5 | Share a resource or case study |
| Follow-up 2 | 10-12 | Social proof: another tutor's experience |
| Follow-up 3 | 21 | Breakup: acknowledge silence, leave door open |

### Angle Rotation

Each follow-up must add genuinely new value. Never just "bump this up."

| Message | Angle | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Initial | Personalized value + soft CTA | Introduce ExamPilot's relevance |
| Follow-up 1 | Different benefit or free resource | Show additional value |
| Follow-up 2 | Social proof or new insight | Build credibility |
| Follow-up 3 (tutors only) | Breakup with door open | Trigger loss aversion |

### The Breakup Message

Removes pressure while creating scarcity through withdrawal. 10-15% response rate from breakup messages.

Structure:
1. Acknowledge you've reached out
2. Validate their potential lack of interest
3. Leave the door open

Example (tutor):
> I haven't heard back, so I'll assume now isn't the right time. Before I close the loop: we just added [new topic coverage] that a few tutors found useful for their Cambridge students. If that changes things, feel free to reply. Otherwise, best of luck this exam season.

### Phrases That Kill Response Rates

- "I never heard back" (12% drop in engagement)
- "Just checking in" (zero value, signals laziness)
- "Bumping this to the top of your inbox" (presumptuous)
- "Did you see my last message?" (guilt-tripping)

## Subject Lines & Opening Lines

### Length: 2-4 Words

- 2-word subject lines get 60% more opens than 5-word
- Mobile truncates at 30-35 characters
- Short = looks personal, not promotional

### The Internal Camouflage Principle

Subject lines that look like they came from a peer, not a brand, double open rates. Recipients mentally categorize before opening.

**Good for ExamPilot email outreach:** "paper 3 mechanics" / "9709 revision" / "exam prep question" / "maths resources"

### Capitalization: Lowercase Wins

All-lowercase has highest open rates. Lowercase looks more personal. For outreach specifically, lowercase beats title case.

### What to Avoid

| Anti-pattern | Impact |
|---|---|
| Salesy language ("boost", "increase", "results") | -17.9% opens |
| Urgency words ("ASAP", "urgent") | Below 36% opens |
| Excessive punctuation ("!!!" or "??") | -36% opens |
| Numbers and percentages in subject | -46% opens |
| Emojis | Hurt professionalism |
| Product name in subject | -57% replies |

### ExamPilot-Specific Openers

**For student DMs (Reddit/Discord):**
- Lead with answering their question or adding to the discussion
- ExamPilot mention only if naturally relevant
- Never open with a pitch

**For tutor emails:**
- Lead with value for their students
- Reference specific exam board or paper
- Keep subject line 2-4 words, lowercase, topic-focused

## Cultural Notes

ExamPilot's audience spans UK and international. Calibrate tone:

| Audience | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| UK students | Casual, peer-level | Avoid formal language. Match Reddit/Discord register. |
| International students | Slightly more formal | Cambridge International students span many cultures |
| UK tutors | Professional but warm | Lead with student outcomes |
| Parents | Trustworthy, clear | Value vs tutoring cost framing. GDPR awareness for under-18s. |
