# Copy Frameworks

Headline formulas, page structures, section types, and writing patterns for ExamPilot marketing copy. Adapted from marketingskills copywriting skill + copy-frameworks + natural-transitions references.

**When to use this:** When writing landing page copy, marketing emails, ad copy, or any persuasive content. Commands `/write-article` and future `/write-landing` reference this guide.

## Headline Formulas

### Outcome-Focused (best for ExamPilot)

| Formula | ExamPilot example |
|---|---|
| {Achieve outcome} without {pain point} | "Ace your A-Level maths without spending EUR80/hour on tutoring" |
| {Achieve outcome} by {how} | "Improve your exam grades by practising the topics you're weakest on" |
| Turn {input} into {outcome} | "Turn revision time into actual exam confidence" |
| {Achieve outcome} in {timeframe} | "Master Cambridge 9709 Paper 1 topics in 20 minutes a day" |

### Problem-Focused

| Formula | ExamPilot example |
|---|---|
| Never {unpleasant event} again | "Never stare at a past paper wondering where to start again" |
| {Question highlighting pain} | "Spending hours revising but still failing practice papers?" |
| Stop {pain}. Start {pleasure}. | "Stop guessing what to revise. Start knowing." |

### Audience-Focused

| Formula | ExamPilot example |
|---|---|
| {Feature} for {audience} | "Adaptive maths practice for A-Level students" |
| You don't have to {skill} to {outcome} | "You don't have to be a natural at maths to get an A" |

### Proof-Focused

| Formula | ExamPilot example |
|---|---|
| [Number] [people] use [product] to [outcome] | "500 students use ExamPilot to prepare for Cambridge 9709" |
| {Key benefit} | "Know exactly what to revise next" |

### Differentiation-Focused

| Formula | ExamPilot example |
|---|---|
| The {opposite} way to {outcome} | "The stress-free way to prepare for A-Level maths" |
| The {category} that {differentiator} | "The revision app that learns your weak spots" |
| Finally, {category} that {benefit} | "Finally, exam prep that adapts to you" |

## Landing Page Structure

### ExamPilot Landing Page (recommended structure)

```
1. Hero (headline, subheadline, CTA, product screenshot)
2. Social proof bar (student count, exam boards supported)
3. Problem section ("Sound familiar?")
4. How it works (3 steps)
5. Key benefits (3, not 10)
6. Student testimonial (with consent)
7. Comparison to alternatives (vs tutoring, vs free resources)
8. FAQ section (4-6 questions)
9. Final CTA with risk reversal (14-day free trial)
```

### Section Writing Guidance

**Hero (above the fold):**
- Headline: outcome-focused formula from above
- Subheadline: who it's for + how it works in one sentence
- CTA: "Start your free trial" (not "Learn more")
- Visual: product screenshot showing the practice loop

**Problem section** openers:
- "You know the feeling..."
- "If you're like most A-Level students..."
- "Spending hours on revision but still not confident?"

Then describe: the specific frustration, time wasted, exam anxiety.

**How it works** (3 steps, numbered):
1. "Choose your exam board" (Cambridge 9709 or Edexcel IAL)
2. "Practice daily" (adaptive questions targeting your weak spots)
3. "Watch your confidence grow" (track progress across every topic)

Each step: simple verb + outcome. No jargon.

**Benefits section** — for each benefit:
- Headline: the outcome they get
- Body: how it works (1-2 sentences)
- Optional proof: number or testimonial snippet

**Testimonial selection** — best testimonials include:
- Specific results ("went from a D to a B in 3 months")
- Before/after context ("I used to spend hours on past papers not knowing what to focus on")
- Role + context for credibility
- Must have consent. Parental consent for under-18s.

**FAQ section:**
- "Is ExamPilot worth it vs free resources like PapaCambridge?"
- "Which exam boards does ExamPilot cover?"
- "How is this different from just doing past papers?"
- "Is there a free trial?"
- "Can my tutor see my progress?"

**Comparison section:**

| | ExamPilot | Private tutoring | Free PDFs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Adaptive practice | Yes | No | No |
| Knows your weak topics | Yes | Sometimes | No |
| Available daily | Yes | 1-2 hours/week | Yes |
| Cost | EUR12-29/mo | EUR40-80/hour | Free |
| Structured revision plan | Yes | Varies | No |

### Page Structure Anti-Patterns

**Feature-heavy (weak):**
```
Hero → Feature 1 → Feature 2 → Feature 3 → CTA
```
This is a list, not a persuasive narrative.

**Varied, engaging (strong):**
```
Hero → Social proof → Problem → How it works → Benefits → Testimonial → Comparison → FAQ → CTA
```
This tells a story and addresses objections.

## Natural Transitions

### For Blog Content and Landing Pages

**Section transitions:**
- "Now let's look at..."
- "With that covered, here's..."
- "Now that you understand X, here's Y."

**Adding evidence:**
- "According to [Source],..."
- "Research shows that..."
- "The numbers confirm..."

**Emphasising:**
- "Here's the key takeaway..."
- "What matters most is..."
- "The bottom line?"

### AI Writing Tells to Avoid

These phrases flag content as AI-generated. Never use them:
- "That being said,..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "At its core,..."
- "In today's digital landscape,..."
- "When it comes to the realm of..."
- "Let's delve into..."
- "In conclusion,..." (at paragraph start)

Also avoid overusing "moreover" and "furthermore". They sound robotic in student-facing content.

### Hedging Language (for claims that need qualification)

- may, might, could
- tends to, generally, typically
- evidence suggests
- in most cases

Use when making exam performance claims that aren't absolute.

## CTA Best Practices

### Primary CTAs for ExamPilot

| Context | CTA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Landing page hero | "Start your free trial" | No CC required. Say so. |
| End of blog article | "Try ExamPilot free for 14 days" | Contextual to the article topic |
| Reddit/community | None or "link in bio" | Never hard-sell on Reddit |
| Tutor outreach | "Want to try it with 2-3 students?" | Low-commitment first ask |
| Email sequence | "Pick up where you left off" | For returning users |

### CTA Rules
- One primary CTA per page/email
- Repeat CTA 2-3 times on landing pages (hero + mid + bottom)
- Risk reversal next to CTA: "14-day free trial. No credit card required."
- Never use "Learn more" as primary CTA (too passive)
