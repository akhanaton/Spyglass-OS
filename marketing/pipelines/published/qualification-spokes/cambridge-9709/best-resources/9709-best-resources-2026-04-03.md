## The Free Resources Every 9709 Student Should Know

Before spending money on anything, make sure you are using these free A-Level maths resources properly. They are genuinely good, and they cover more ground than most students realise.

### PapaCambridge -- The Past Papers Archive

If you are revising for Cambridge 9709, you will end up on [PapaCambridge](https://papacambridge.com/) eventually. It hosts the most complete free archive of past papers, mark schemes, and examiner reports for every 9709 variant going back years.

**What it does well.** Complete paper archives across every exam session. Topical question compilations that let you drill a single topic across multiple papers. Mark schemes that show exactly how examiners allocate marks step by step.

**What it does not do.** PapaCambridge is a filing cabinet, not a teacher. There are no explanations, no worked solutions, and no way to track what you have practised or how you performed. The site is also heavy on ads -- Rajan in Dubai described it as "trying to revise while someone waves a billboard in your face." When your May exam is three weeks away and you are working through a past paper at 11pm, intrusive ads are more than an annoyance. They break your concentration at exactly the moment you need it most.

**Best for:** Phase 2 revision, when you already understand the content and need exam-style practice. Not useful if you are still learning the material.

**Cost:** Free.

If you are looking for [best A-Level maths resources](https://www.exampilot.io/waitlist) that go beyond static PDFs and 9709 revision websites, keep reading -- but do not skip PapaCambridge. Past papers are non-negotiable for 9709 prep.

### Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT)

PMT is probably the most-visited revision site among A-Level students in the UK. It has a massive library of worksheets, cheat sheets, and practice questions. And it is free.

**What it does well.** The sheer volume of content is impressive. Revision notes are clearly laid out, and the worksheets give you plenty of practice. For UK exam boards -- Edexcel, AQA, OCR -- PMT is genuinely excellent. Nearly 5.8 million unique users visit annually. That is not an accident.

**What it does not do for CIE students.** Here is the problem most best A-Level maths websites lists will not tell you: PMT has essentially zero content specific to Cambridge 9709. The maths overlaps in places, but the syllabus structure, question style, and mark scheme conventions are different. Cambridge questions are phrased differently. The mark allocation follows different patterns. If you are using PMT Edexcel worksheets to revise for 9709, you are adapting content that was not designed for your exam.

PMT also crashes under traffic during exam season -- a recurring complaint on The Student Room and Trustpilot. Video ads are heavy. Worked solutions are absent for most questions, replaced with mark schemes only. Trustpilot reviews sit at 3.6 out of 5 from 17 reviews -- functional, but not loved.

**Best for:** Extra practice if you have exhausted CIE-specific resources. Useful for topic understanding, but treat the questions as supplementary, not primary revision.

**Cost:** Free (ad-supported).

### Cambridge International -- The Official Source

The most underused free resource for 9709 students is the one Cambridge gives you directly.

[Cambridge International](https://www.cambridgeinternational.org/programmes-and-qualifications/cambridge-international-as-and-a-level-mathematics-9709/) publishes specimen papers, syllabus documents, and -- critically -- examiner reports for every exam session. Those reports tell you exactly what students got wrong and what examiners were looking for.

Amara in Nairobi started reading examiner reports three weeks before her exam. She noticed that examiners repeatedly flagged students who did not show the chain rule step in [differentiation questions](/cambridge/9709/pure-1/differentiation/). She made sure she showed it every time. Her Paper 1 mark jumped from a projected C to a solid B. Not because she learned new maths, but because she stopped losing marks she did not need to lose.

**Best for:** Understanding what examiners actually want. The syllabus tells you the topics. The examiner reports tell you the traps. If you are not reading these, you are leaving marks on the table. Read our [9709 common mistakes guide](/cambridge/9709/common-mistakes/) for the patterns that come up every session.

**Cost:** Free.

## Paid Revision Platforms

### Save My Exams

Save My Exams is the most popular paid platform for CIE students, and it deserves that reputation. It offers structured revision notes, topic-by-topic exam questions, and difficulty grading -- all mapped to the Cambridge 9709 syllabus.

**What it does well.** The CIE-specific content is strong. Notes are clearly written, questions are graded by difficulty, and the topic structure follows the syllabus closely. Their Smart Mark AI marking tool is a recent addition that provides instant feedback on answers. Trustpilot reviews sit at 4.6 out of 5 from nearly 1,900 reviews -- genuinely high. That rating reflects real value.

**What it does not do.** The free tier is extremely limited -- one topic question set and five revision notes per month. Full access runs approximately 60 pounds per year. For students in Pakistan, Nigeria, or Malaysia, that is a significant spend relative to local purchasing power. A scholarship programme offers 10 free annual accounts per month, but that does not scale to the hundreds of thousands of CIE students who need affordable access.

The main complaint across student forums and reviews: pricing versus free alternatives. The content is good, but whether it is worth 60 pounds depends on your budget and what you would use instead.

Save My Exams also publishes its own "alternatives" pages -- comparisons against PMT, StudySmarter, and others -- that unsurprisingly conclude it is the best option. PMT does the same thing in reverse. That is marketing, not comparison.

**Best for:** Students who want structured, CIE-specific revision notes and can afford the subscription. A genuine save my exams alternative is hard to find because few platforms match its Cambridge-specific depth.

**Cost:** Approximately 60 pounds per year for full access. 7-day free trial available. Free tier is too limited for serious revision.

### Other Paid Options

**TutorChase:** Online tutoring marketplace. Good if you want one-to-one help, but it is a tutoring service, not a self-study platform. Prices vary by tutor.

**Textbooks (Hugh Neill, Cambridge University Press):** The official Cambridge textbook is solid for first-time learning. The Cambridge endorsed revision guide is well structured. Less useful for focused exam revision and sometimes unavailable or expensive to ship outside the UK.

**Integral Maths (MEI):** A comprehensive teaching platform used by UK schools. Requires a school subscription -- not available to individual students. Covers UK boards, not Cambridge International.

## Video Resources

### ExamSolutions

If you are stuck on a concept, [ExamSolutions on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@ExamSolutions) is where students go. The channel walks through exam questions step by step, starting from first principles.

Students recommend ExamSolutions more consistently than any other video resource for A-Level maths. The style is methodical -- sometimes slow, but that is the point. When you are confused about why the chain rule gives you that extra factor, or why [integration](/cambridge/9709/pure-1/integration/) of (2x + 3)^4 requires dividing by the inner coefficient, slow and clear is exactly what you need.

**Best for:** Understanding methods when textbook explanations do not click. ExamSolutions has earned its reputation across student forums, Reddit, and The Student Room.

### TLMaths

TLMaths organises content by the new specification and is particularly good for Pure Mathematics topics. The production quality is higher than most education channels, and the playlist structure makes it easy to find specific topics without scrolling through hundreds of unrelated videos.

**Best for:** Systematic video revision alongside written notes. Good structure, clear explanations.

### When Video Helps and When It Does Not

Video is excellent for understanding methods. It is poor for building exam speed.

Carlos in Madrid watched every ExamSolutions video on integration before his November session. He understood every method perfectly. Then he sat a timed past paper and ran out of time on question 10, leaving 12 marks on the table. The problem was not comprehension -- it was that watching someone solve an integration problem is not the same as solving one yourself under timed conditions.

Use video to learn the methods. Then switch to practice. Timed practice. With a clock running. That is where you build the speed and confidence that translates to exam marks.

## What Is Missing From Every Resource Above

Here is what none of the resources above provide -- free or paid:

**No predicted grade.** You have no idea whether your current performance translates to an A, a C, or a U until results day. You might feel ready. You might not be.

**No adaptive practice.** Every platform gives you the same questions regardless of whether you are scoring 90% or 40% on a topic. A student who has mastered [quadratics](/cambridge/9709/pure-1/quadratics/) gets the same question set as one who is struggling with basic factorisation. That is wasted time for one and overwhelming for the other.

**No AI tutoring.** When you are stuck at midnight in Karachi, there is no one to ask. Your teacher is asleep. Your tutor's next session is Thursday. The textbook explanation did not click the first time, and re-reading it is not going to help.

**No spaced repetition.** You revise integration today, forget [series and circular measure](/cambridge/9709/pure-1/series/) next week, and wonder why your mock exam goes badly. Without a system that tracks what you are about to forget and resurfaces it, you are relying on luck and repetition.

**No CIE-specific question engine.** PMT gives you Edexcel questions. PapaCambridge gives you past papers you will eventually run out of. Neither generates new exam-style questions mapped to the 9709 syllabus.

These are not minor gaps. They are the difference between revision that feels productive and revision that actually is.
