## Can Cambridge 9709 Students Use PMT?

Yes -- with clear boundaries. Is Physics & Maths Tutor good for CIE students? The short answer: good for maths practice, not for exam preparation.

The underlying mathematics in Edexcel A-Level and Cambridge 9709 [Pure 1](/cambridge/9709/pure-1/) overlaps significantly. Algebra, differentiation, integration, coordinate geometry -- the core content is largely the same. PMT's Edexcel past papers are legitimate practice material for the maths itself.

Here is where it breaks down:

- **Exam style differs.** Cambridge 9709 questions are structured differently from Edexcel. The way marks are allocated, the phrasing of "show that" and "hence" questions, and the expected level of working all vary between boards.
- **Mark scheme conventions differ.** What earns a method mark on an Edexcel paper is not always what earns one on a Cambridge paper. If you're practising Edexcel mark schemes, you're training yourself in the wrong conventions.
- **Topic weighting differs.** Cambridge examiners emphasise different subtopics compared to Edexcel. Preparing based on Edexcel's question distribution means you might over-prepare in some areas and under-prepare in others.
- **Examiner report intelligence is absent.** Cambridge publishes examiner reports that reveal common mistakes and what examiners look for. PMT doesn't analyse these because it doesn't cover CIE.

Think of it this way: practising Edexcel questions on PMT is like training for a driving test in Manchester by practising the routes in London. You're definitely improving your driving. But you're not learning the specific roundabouts and junctions the examiner will test you on.
