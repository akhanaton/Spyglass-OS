## Save My Exams at a Glance

Save My Exams is one of the most popular revision platforms for Cambridge International students. It covers CIE, Edexcel, AQA, OCR, and IB. You get structured revision notes, topic-sorted exam questions, mark schemes, and flashcards.

The platform is well-organised. Notes are clean, readable, and mapped to the syllabus. If you want a single place to read through every topic before your exam, it does the job.

**Pricing** is where things get complicated. There is a free tier, but it limits you to one topic question and five revision notes per month. Barely enough for a single Pure 1 topic. The full experience costs around 60 pounds per year.

**Trustpilot** paints a mostly positive picture, [4.6 out of 5 from 1,848 reviews](https://uk.trustpilot.com/review/savemyexams.com). Most students praise the note quality. Most complaints centre on the aggressive paywall. Several reviewers on [The Student Room](https://www.thestudentroom.co.uk/) also note that questions do not always match real Cambridge exam style.

Credit where it is due. Save My Exams does several things well, and in some areas, better than ExamPilot.

**Broader exam board coverage.** If you're taking AQA, OCR, or IB alongside your CIE subjects, Save My Exams covers all of them in one subscription. ExamPilot doesn't cover those boards and has no plans to in the near term. For multi-board students, this matters.

**Comprehensive, well-structured notes.** Save My Exams has been building its content library for years. The revision notes are thorough, clearly written, and organised by topic and difficulty. If you learn best by reading structured notes before attempting questions, this is a genuine strength.

**Established and proven.** Nearly 1,850 Trustpilot reviews and years of content development mean the platform is battle-tested. Thousands of students have used it successfully. That track record counts for something.

## Who Should Use What?

There's no single right answer here. The best choice depends on where you are in your revision and what problem you're trying to solve.

**Use Save My Exams if** you want comprehensive revision notes organised by topic and exam board, especially if you're taking multiple qualifications. The notes are genuinely good. If structured reading is how you learn best, the 60 pounds per year is reasonable.

**Use ExamPilot if** you want to know your predicted grade, practise questions targeted to your specific weak areas, and have an AI tutor available when you're stuck. This is for students who've done the reading and need to convert that knowledge into exam performance.

**Use both if** you want the best of each approach. Read through Save My Exams notes for topic understanding. Then use ExamPilot for adaptive practice, spaced repetition, and a clear picture of your exam readiness. They are complementary more than competitive.

**Use neither if** free resources cover your needs. ## Is Save My Exams Worth 60 Pounds a Year?

This is the question behind every save my exams review, so here is an honest answer.

**For structured revision notes, yes.** The notes are well-written, exam-board specific, and save you hours of compiling your own. If you'd otherwise spend that time searching across multiple free sites, 60 pounds for a year of organised content is fair.

**For exam practice, it depends.** The questions are useful, but they're static. Every student gets the same set, with no adaptation to your level or retention tracking. Students on The Student Room regularly point out that the topical questions don't always match the style of real Cambridge papers. If practice is your primary need, there are strong alternatives, both free and paid.

**For knowing if you're actually ready, no.** Save My Exams doesn't offer a predicted grade, adaptive difficulty, or spaced repetition. It tells you what to study. It doesn't tell you whether you've studied enough. That's a different problem, and it requires a different tool.

Here's the scenario that makes this clear. Fatima in Dubai has been working through Save My Exams notes on differentiation for two weeks. She's read every page and completed every available question. She feels prepared.

But she has no way of knowing whether she'll remember chain rule applications in three weeks. She doesn't know if her accuracy under timed conditions is strong enough for a B. Save My Exams can't answer that question. A tool with a predicted grade and spaced repetition can.
