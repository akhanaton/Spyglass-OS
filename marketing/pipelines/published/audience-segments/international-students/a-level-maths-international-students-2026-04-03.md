## Revision Planning by Exam Session

One of the biggest gaps in existing revision advice is the assumption that everyone sits exams in May/June. For international students, that's often not the case.

### May/June Session (Exam Window: April-June)

This is the session most online resources target. If you're sitting May/June, you can follow broadly standard timelines -- but adapted to your own school calendar rather than UK terms.

**February**: Begin structured topic-by-topic revision. Work through each Pure 1 and Pure 2 topic systematically. Use the 9709 syllabus checklist to track coverage.

**March-April**: Start timed past paper practice. Work through at least eight full papers under exam conditions. Review every mistake against the mark scheme -- not just the answer, but the method marks.

**April-May**: Targeted gap-filling. Use your past paper results to identify your weakest two or three topics and focus revision there.

**A note on Ramadan**: In some years, Ramadan overlaps with the May/June revision window. If you're fasting, plan your hardest study sessions around suhoor or after iftar, when energy levels are higher.

Short, focused sessions of 25-30 minutes work better than long blocks during fasting hours.

### October/November Session (Exam Window: October-November)

Ahmed in Dubai sits the November session because it aligns with his school's academic calendar. But when he searched for "november session a level revision" plans online, he found nothing. Every guide, every timeline, every countdown assumed May/June.

If you're a November session student, here's what you need to know.

**The exams are the same standard.** Same syllabus, same question style, same mark scheme conventions, same grade boundary approach. You're not at a disadvantage for sitting the later session.

**August**: Begin structured revision. This is your equivalent of February for May/June students.

**September**: Past paper phase. Work through papers from both May/June and October/November sessions -- they are interchangeable for practice purposes.

**October**: Final targeted revision on weak areas. Use examiner reports to understand common errors.

The past papers, the mark schemes, and the examiner reports all apply equally to both sessions. Cambridge doesn't create a "harder" or "easier" paper for November.

## Exam Strategy for International Students

The mark scheme is the same worldwide. There are no regional differences, no country-specific papers, no adjusted grade boundaries. A student in Lagos and a student in London sit papers of the same difficulty and are graded to the same standard.

This is important because it means every piece of exam strategy content applies equally to you.

**Show your working.** Cambridge awards method marks for correct working even if your final answer is wrong. A correct answer without working shown can score zero marks. This rule applies everywhere, every session.

**Read the examiner reports.** Cambridge publishes these for free after every session. They tell you exactly what mistakes candidates made and what the examiners were looking for. These are the single most underused resource in 9709 revision.

**Know the command words.** "Hence" means use the previous result. "Otherwise" means you can use any valid method. "State" means no working is needed.

These conventions are consistent across all sessions and centres.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are grade boundaries the same in every country?

Yes. Cambridge sets global grade boundaries for 9709. A student in Pakistan, the UAE, Malaysia, or Nigeria is graded to exactly the same standard as a student in the UK.

### Is the November session easier than May/June?

No. Both sessions follow the same syllabus, the same question style, and the same difficulty standard. Your november session a level revision should follow the same approach as May/June preparation.

Cambridge doesn't make one session easier than the other. Grade boundaries may vary slightly between sessions, but that reflects the difficulty of that specific paper -- not a systematic difference.

### Can I use UK resources for Cambridge 9709?

Partially. The underlying mathematics is the same, so concept explanations from UK-focused sites can be useful. But exam technique, mark scheme conventions, and question style differ between Cambridge and UK boards like Edexcel or AQA. For exam practice, always use 9709-specific resources.

### Is Cambridge 9709 harder than Edexcel A-Level Maths?

Different, not harder. The mathematical content is at the same depth. The difference is in exam structure -- Cambridge papers tend to have fewer questions with more marks each, requiring extended problem-solving. Neither is objectively harder; they test differently.

### Do I need a tutor to get an A*?

No. Structured self-study with the right resources is sufficient. Many A* candidates, particularly in countries where private tutoring is expensive, achieve their grades through disciplined independent practice with past papers, mark schemes, and adaptive tools like ExamPilot.
