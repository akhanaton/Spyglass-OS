# ExamPilot Teacher & School Voice Guide

Voice for all teacher and school-facing content: outreach messages, partnership pages, teacher onboarding, LinkedIn engagement, and school recommendation materials. ExamPilot is always consumer-purchased. Teachers and schools are referral partners, not customers.

---

## The Voice in One Sentence

A professional colleague who values teachers' expertise and offers a complementary tool that makes their students perform better.

---

## Three Voice Pillars

### 1. Professional Partnership
Address teachers as the expert in the room. ExamPilot complements their teaching. It does not replace it, compete with it, or suggest they are insufficient.

**Do:** "Your students get personalised gap analysis between sessions. You get visibility into exactly where each student needs help."
**Avoid:** "ExamPilot teaches better than..." or any framing that positions the product as a substitute.

### 2. Student Outcomes
Teachers care about tools that make their students perform better because student performance reflects on them. Lead with student outcomes, not product features.

**Do:** "Students using ExamPilot can identify their weakest 9709 topics before the mock exam."
**Avoid:** Feature dumps, technical architecture, pricing. Teachers want to know: will this help my students?

### 3. Zero Admin Burden
Teachers are time-poor. Any tool that creates work will be rejected. ExamPilot should be something they recommend once, then students self-serve.

**Do:** "Share one link with your class. Students sign up individually. No admin on your end."
**Avoid:** Implying teachers need to manage accounts, track usage, or generate reports.

## Tone by Content Type

| Content Type | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Initial outreach (LinkedIn/email) | Professional, respectful, low-commitment ask | "I'm building an adaptive revision tool specifically for Cambridge 9709. Would you be open to having 2-3 of your students try it? I'd value your feedback." |
| Follow-up | Brief, value-reinforcing, no pressure | "Just wanted to share: the first 3 students who tried it identified integration as their weakest topic within one session." |
| Teacher dashboard / onboarding | Efficient, clear, benefit-oriented | "Your dashboard shows aggregate class performance by topic. No individual student data without their consent." |
| School recommendation materials | Credibility-first, outcomes-focused | "ExamPilot uses spaced repetition and knowledge mapping to help Cambridge 9709 students identify and close knowledge gaps before exams." |

## Messaging Framework

### Core Value Proposition (Teacher)
"Your students get personalised gap analysis between sessions. You get insight into where they need help most. Zero admin required."

### Key Messages

**Message 1 -- Value for Their Students**
"ExamPilot maps each student's knowledge against the 9709 specification topic by topic. They know exactly what to focus on before the next lesson."

**Message 2 -- Value for the Teacher**
"See aggregate class performance by topic. Know which areas to prioritise in your teaching without guessing or waiting for the exam."

**Message 3 -- Zero Friction**
"Share a link. Students sign up individually. Parents pay consumer pricing. No school procurement, no licensing, no admin."

**Message 4 -- Professional Credibility**
"Built on learning science: spaced repetition (FSRS), knowledge mapping, and misconception detection. Designed to complement teaching, not replace it."

## Teacher Referral Program Framing

**What teachers get:**
- Free premium access to ExamPilot for personal use in lesson prep
- Aggregate class performance dashboard (with student consent)
- No cost, no contract, no obligation

**What we ask:**
- "Would you be open to having 2-3 of your students try it for free?"
- Low-commitment first ask. Let the product prove itself through student outcomes.

**Scaling:**
- 20+ students via teacher's referral link = extended free access + recognition
- Teacher referral tracked via UTM: `?utm_source=teacher&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=[teacher-name]`

## Outreach Rules

- Only warm intros or professional channels (LinkedIn, teacher WhatsApp groups, conference connections)
- Maximum 1 follow-up if no response. Do not chase.
- Never cold DM teachers on personal social media
- Never mention competitors negatively
- Never imply their current teaching is insufficient
- Always frame as "complementary tool" not "solution to a problem"
- Track all outreach in Attio CRM: contact, school, school size, status, students attributed

---

## What We Never Do (Teacher/School Content)

- B2B pricing or licensing language ("school plan", "institutional rate", "per-seat pricing")
- Position ExamPilot as a tutor or teacher replacement
- Cold spam or mass outreach
- Promise specific grade improvements
- Share individual student data with teachers without explicit student consent
- Use student-casual voice (no slang, no peer-level register)
- Use parent-worry framing (teachers are professionals, not anxious stakeholders)
- Suggest teachers need ExamPilot to do their job well
