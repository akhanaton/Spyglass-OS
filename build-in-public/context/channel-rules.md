# X / Build in Public — Channel Rules

Operational rules for ExamPilot's build-in-public presence on X. For strategy context, see `build-in-public/references/x-strategy.md`.

## X / Build in Public

**Status:** Active. Personal accounts only (Enitan + Teresa).

**Target audience:** Founders, indie hackers, edtech builders, potential advisors. NOT students.

**Strategy reference:** `build-in-public/references/x-strategy.md`

**Rules of engagement:**
- Both founders post from personal accounts. Never a brand account.
- Every draft reviewed before posting. No auto-publish. No exceptions.
- Use `references/voice-{author}.md` for voice, NOT voice-house.md.
- No links in main tweets (deprioritised by X algorithm). Links go in self-reply.
- One hashtag max (#buildinpublic), only when relevant, never in every post.
- No em dashes, no banned phrases (see content-standards.md).
- Reply to every comment on your posts within 2 hours.
- 70% replies, 30% original content in first 60 days. Shift to 50/50 after 500 followers.

**Posting cadence:** 1-2 original tweets/day + 2-3 quality replies. 1 weekly thread (Friday draft, Monday/Tuesday publish).

**Best posting times:** Test and adjust. Start with 8-9 AM GMT and 5-6 PM GMT.

**Content sources:** decisions/log.md, Linear issues, PostHog metrics, git commits, wiki articles, signal-review output, marketing experiments.

**Content split:**
- Enitan: engineering, product architecture, data/metrics, technical build-in-public, Spyglass OS
- Teresa: marketing experiments, content strategy, community building, educational insights

**What gets ignored:**
- Threads without a specific number or screenshot
- AI-sounding prose (audience is hypersensitive)
- Product pitches disguised as transparency
- Same content posted by both founders

**Scheduling:** Via Postiz (Standard plan, $29/mo). Human queues drafts after review. Never API-direct (Phase 0).

**Metrics:** Track via Postiz analytics + UTM links into PostHog. Weekly: impressions, engagement rate, follower growth, reply ratio. Monthly: content type performance review.

## LinkedIn / Professional Authority

**Status:** Active. Teresa's personal account only.

**Target audience:** CIE/Edexcel maths teachers at international schools, parents of international school students, edtech professionals. NOT students. NOT the indie hacker audience.

**Strategy reference:** `build-in-public/references/linkedin-strategy.md`

**Rules of engagement:**
- Teresa posts from her personal account. Never a brand account.
- Every draft reviewed by Teresa before posting. No auto-publish.
- Use `references/voice-teresa.md` for voice (or `voice-house.md` as base if voice-teresa.md not yet populated).
- No links in post body — LinkedIn deprioritises them. Put links in the first comment and say "link in comments" at end of post.
- 3 hashtags max at the end of posts. Relevant: #cambridgeinternational #alevel #mathseducation #examprep #edtech
- No banned phrases (see linkedin-strategy.md).
- Reply to all comments within 2 hours of posting.
- Do not cross-post from X. Adapt or rewrite — never copy-paste.
- Engage with comments in first 30-60 minutes after posting (algorithm timing).

**Posting cadence:** 2-3 original posts per week. Quality over frequency. LinkedIn penalises inconsistency more than low volume.

**Best posting times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am GMT. Avoid Mondays and Friday afternoons.

**Content sources:** decisions/log.md (educator-angle adaptation), Linear issues (student outcome focus), PostHog metrics (learning behaviour insights), wiki articles, marketing experiments (what we learned about student engagement).

**The angle rule:** Same source material as X, different frame. X = founder confession. LinkedIn = educator insight. Always state the frame shift explicitly before drafting (see `/write-linkedin` Step 3).

**Content split:**
- Teresa: educational insights, behind-the-scenes educator angle, teacher recognition, milestone announcements with professional framing
- Enitan: joins later with product/engineering angle (when relevant to professional audience)

**Scheduling:** Via Postiz (Standard plan, $29/mo — same as X). Incremental cost: $0. Teresa copies approved drafts to Postiz manually (Phase 0).

**Metrics:** Postiz dashboard + LinkedIn native analytics + UTM links into PostHog. Weekly: impressions, engagement rate, connection growth. Monthly: teacher outreach acceptance rate, inbound DMs from target audience. Reviewed in `/weekly-pulse` (Step 6.6).
