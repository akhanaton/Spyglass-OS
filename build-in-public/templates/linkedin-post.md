## Post Types

**insight:** Educational observation about how students learn, fail, or pass CIE maths. Sourced from Teresa's experience or ExamPilot data.

**milestone:** Professional announcement. Not "we shipped X" (X voice) — "We reached Y, which matters because Z for students."

**teaching-moment:** Something the process of building ExamPilot revealed about education. Adapted from decisions/log.md, reframed as educator insight.

**educator-recognition:** Celebrate what excellent CIE maths teachers do. No pitch. Pure goodwill.

**behind-the-scenes:** Professional angle on the building process. "What we got wrong" or "What surprised us" — for an audience of educators, not founders.

## Format Rules

- **Hook in first 2 lines.** LinkedIn shows ~210 characters before "see more". The hook must earn the click.
- **Line breaks between paragraphs.** No walls of text. One idea per paragraph.
- **1,000-1,500 characters optimal** for maximum reach (studies consistently show this range).
- **No links in post body.** LinkedIn deprioritises external links. Put the link in the first comment and say "link in comments" at the end of the post.
- **3 hashtags max**, at the end. Relevant: #cambridgeinternational #alevel #mathseducation #examprep #edtech #igcse #internationalschools
- **No banned phrases.** See linkedin-strategy.md for full list.
- **At least one specific detail:** a student observation, a data point, a named topic (9709, Paper 1), or a concrete outcome.

## Hook Patterns (use sparingly — rotate)

- Start with a counter-intuitive claim: "Most CIE students revise wrong."
- Start with a number: "3 things I didn't expect when we built [X]."
- Start with a question: "Why do students who understand integration still fail Paper 1?"
- Start with a short observation: "A student told me something last week that I haven't stopped thinking about."

Never start with "I" (LinkedIn algorithm de-ranks it). Never start with "Excited to share."
