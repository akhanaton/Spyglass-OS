## When to use this format

Native LinkedIn articles are SEO-indexed by Google and have a longer shelf life than posts. Use for:
- Deep educational content that warrants 800-1,500 words
- Thought leadership pieces Teresa wants to be found for professionally
- Content that mirrors or extends an ExamPilot blog article (different angle, not duplicate)
- Quarterly — not weekly. These are investments, not regular posts.

## Structure

**Title:** Specific and useful. "Why CIE Maths Students Struggle with Integration (And What Actually Helps)" not "Thoughts on Exam Prep."

**Opening (150 words):** The problem. Who this is for. Why it matters. No "let me introduce myself" preamble.

**Body (600-1,000 words):** H2 sections. One idea per section. Short paragraphs. Specific examples. Student observations (anonymised). Data if available.

**Closing (100-150 words):** One actionable takeaway. Soft reference to ExamPilot if directly relevant — never forced. CTA: follow for more, or "if you're a teacher and want to discuss this, let's connect."

## Rules

- No links in body that feel promotional. Educational external links (e.g., CIE syllabus) are fine.
- Write in Teresa's voice, not ExamPilot brand voice.
- Flag all exam-board-specific claims with [VERIFY] before publishing.
- Minimum one concrete student scenario or data point per section.
- No "in conclusion" or "to summarise." Just end it.
- Not a sales page. ExamPilot can be mentioned once, naturally, if directly relevant.
